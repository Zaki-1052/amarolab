# Subgroup Meetings

# Explanation of Subgroups[edit](</mediawiki/index.php?title=Subgroup_Meetings&action=edit&section=1> "Edit section: Explanation of Subgroups")]

The Amaro lab subgroups provide a collaborative friendly environment where lab members can discuss their research and science. These meetings are not a forum to present formal research presentations of completed projects, that is what lab meeting is for. Instead, these meetings are a forum for discussing work in progress and getting feedback on the work. Subgroup meeting are intended to be guided discussion where one member presents their research or a paper and leads a discussion about the work. Subgroup meetings should have one of the following formats, listed in descending priority. 

1) **Work in Progress Research Presentation** : In this meeting a member presents their current or planed research. The group provides feedback on the work. 

2) **Journal Club** : A member of the group leads a discussion of a research paper of importance to the group. These papers should be examples of high quality research. 

3) **Methods Tutorial** : A member of the group presents on a new method they have learned with the group. They teach the group members the method and leads a discussion about how to use the method in their research. 

4) **Practice Talk** : A member of the group presents a talk that will be giving at a meeting, etc. The group provides feedback on the talk. 

There are currently two active subgroups in the Amaro lab Dynamics and Markov State Models (DMSM) and Drug Discovery (DD). A subgroup meeting is held every Thursday at 3:00 PM with each group meeting every other week. While we encourage lab members to attend whichever subgroups they are interested in, and attending all subgroups is a good choice, Rommie asks that you select one group as you "home" subgroup. Place you name in Membership list below.
