Most or all of these can be found in the shelves in the lab. If you can't find them, Dr. V. might have them in his office or someone might be borrowing it. You might be able to find a book in the library, and I highly encourage you to use the Interlibrary Loan system to order any books you might need. 

### Required[edit](</mediawiki/index.php?title=ReadingList&action=edit&section=2> "Edit section: Required")]

  * None



### Recommended[edit](</mediawiki/index.php?title=ReadingList&action=edit&section=3> "Edit section: Recommended")]

  * _Practical Python_ by Magnus Lie Hetland
  * _Molecular Driving Forces_ by Ken Dill 
    * selected chapters about kinetics, diffusion, and electrostatics
  * _Molecular Modeling_
    * selected chapters about molecular mechanics and molecular dynamics
  * _Computational Chemistry_
    * selected chapters about molecular mechanics and molecular dynamics
  * _The Principles of Statistical Mechanics by Richard Tolman_
    * Chapter 2 is extremely helpful for understanding Langevin dynamics.
    * Reference the lecture notes ["Introduction to the calculus of variations"](<https://www.open.edu/openlearn/ocw/pluginfile.php/1118521/mod_resource/content/3/Introduction%20to%20the%20calculus%20of%20variations_ms327.pdf>) as needed



### Suggested[edit](</mediawiki/index.php?title=ReadingList&action=edit&section=4> "Edit section: Suggested")]

  * Remaining chapters in any of the books mentioned above
  * _Feynman's Lectures in Physics_ by Richard Feynman
  * _Statistical Mechanics_ by Donald McQuarrie
  * _Mathematical Methods in Physics_ by Arfken



## Articles[edit](</mediawiki/index.php?title=ReadingList&action=edit&section=5> "Edit section: Articles")]

Some of these articles can be found online for free. Sometimes, you might be asked to pay for an article. **You should never have to pay for an article while you are a student at this university**. The library is supposed to be able to help us obtain any articles that we need at no cost to us. If you can't find access to the article, either ask the library, check SciHub, or you can email the corresponding author to ask for access. 

### Required[edit](</mediawiki/index.php?title=ReadingList&action=edit&section=6> "Edit section: Required")]

  * **Molecular Dynamics and Drug Discovery** by Durrant and McCammon: <https://bmcbiol.biomedcentral.com/articles/10.1186/1741-7007-9-71>
  * **Multiscale Estimation of Binding Kinetics Using Brownian Dynamics, Molecular Dynamics and Milestoning** by Votapka and Amaro: <http://journals.plos.org/ploscompbiol/article?id=10.1371/journal.pcbi.1004381>
  * **SEEKR: Simulation Enabled Estimation of Kinetic Rates, A Computational Tool to Estimate Molecular Kinetics and Its Application to Trypsin–Benzamidine Binding** by Votapka, Jagger, Heyneman and Amaro: <https://pubs.acs.org/doi/abs/10.1021/acs.jpcb.6b09388>
  * **OpenMM 7: Rapid development of high performance algorithms for molecular dynamics** by Eastman et. al.: <http://journals.plos.org/ploscompbiol/article?id=10.1371/journal.pcbi.1005659>
  * **BrownDye: a Software Package for Brownian Dynamics** by Huber and McCammon: <https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pmc/articles/PMC2994412/>
  * Structure and function papers relevant to your molecular system(s)



  


### Recommended[edit](</mediawiki/index.php?title=ReadingList&action=edit&section=7> "Edit section: Recommended")]

  * Ermak, DL and McCammon, JA. **Brownian Dynamics with Hydrodynamic Interactions** , J. Chem. Phys. 69, 1352-1360 (1978)
  * Northrup SH, Allison SA and McCammon JA. **Brownian Dynamics Simulation of Diffusion-Influenced Bimolecular Reactions** J. Chem. Phys. 80, 1517-1526
  * Elber, R et. al. **Computing time scales from reaction coordinates by milestoning** , J. Chem. Phys. 2004: <https://aip.scitation.org/doi/abs/10.1063/1.1738640>
  * Vanden-Eijnden et. al. **On the Assumptions Underlying Milestoning** , J. Chem. Phys. : <https://aip.scitation.org/doi/abs/10.1063/1.2996509>
  * Vanden-Eijnden et. al. **Markovian milestoning with Voronoi tessellations** , J. Chem. Phys. 2009: <https://doi.org/10.1063/1.3129843>
  * Majek et. al. "Milestoning Without a Reaction Coordinate**, J. Chem. Theory & Comp.: <https://pubs.acs.org/doi/abs/10.1021/ct100114j>**
  * **Software Development Best Practices for Computational Chemistry** by Chodera et. al.: <https://github.com/choderalab/software-development>
